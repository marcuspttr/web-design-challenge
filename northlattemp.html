Analysis: Max Temperature


As we would expect, the further north of the equator you moved the lower the max temperature experienced. The linear regression produced a correlation coeffcient of r = -0.73, showing a moderately strong predictive relationship between the variables. An interesting observation can be seen as the y-intercept predicts a max temperature of 92 degrees farenheit at cities on the equator (our presumptive hottest average point); however, there are plenty of points for cities within 20-30 degrees latitude experiencing much higher temperatures. Looking into these potential outliers would be an interesting exploration of this topic.
